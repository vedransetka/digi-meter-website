Project Overview

Measuring Digital Competence for Resilient Learning Ecosystems

DIGI-METER responds to a key challenge in European education systems: while the DigComp 2.2 framework clearly defines the competences citizens need in digital environments, its practical use in schools and vocational education and training is still fragmented. DIGI-METER helps bridge this gap by translating DigComp 2.2 into measurable indicators, practical assessment tools, and clear feedback for learners, educators, and institutions.

11 Partners · 8 EU Countries
DigComp 2.2 · AI-Powered
What We're Building

AI-Supported Tools for Schools, VET Providers & Policymakers

Through the project, partners will develop AI-supported self-assessment tools, a teacher dashboard, and open resources that help schools, VET providers, and policymakers better understand, monitor, and improve digital skills. The tools are designed to support personalised learning, evidence-based decision-making, and more consistent digital competence assessment across different educational contexts.

Ethics-by-Design Approach

A strong focus is placed on ethics, inclusion, accessibility, and data protection. DIGI-METER follows an ethics-by-design approach, with attention to transparency, GDPR compliance, fairness, explainability, and user control.

DIGI-METER Adaptive Platform

Currently in active development, the secure platform will provide a personalized dashboard utilizing adaptive testing engines to assess digital competences.

  • Learner Self-Assessment: Dynamic question paths based on real-time responses to accurately assess skills without fatigue.
  • Teacher Dashboard Panel: Aggregated class analytics to identify skill gaps, monitor progress, and suggest learning materials.
  • Trustworthy & Inclusive: Designed for WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility, GDPR compliance, and inclusive use.
