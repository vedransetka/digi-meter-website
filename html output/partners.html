Who We Are

Each partner contributes a distinct area of expertise. Together we cover the full cycle from framework design through technical development, piloting, policy transfer, and communication.

Center for Social Innovation (CSI)
🇨🇾 Cyprus
Coordinator

Center for Social Innovation (CSI) is a research and development organisation based in Cyprus, focused on fostering positive social change through innovation, education, technology, and collaboration. CSI works across local, European, and international projects, developing solutions that address societal challenges in areas such as digital transformation, education and training, social inclusion, entrepreneurship, sustainability, and youth empowerment. Through research-driven methodologies and strong stakeholder engagement, CSI designs and implements innovative tools, training programmes, policy recommendations, and awareness initiatives that strengthen communities and support lifelong learning. The organisation actively participates in Erasmus+, Horizon Europe, and other EU-funded programmes, collaborating with universities, public authorities, NGOs, and private-sector partners to promote social impact, digital resilience, and inclusive growth. CSI's mission is to bridge innovation and society by creating practical, sustainable, and human-centred solutions.

Project Coordination — Project coordination, quality assurance, reporting, sustainability support, and overall consortium coordination.

Lascò specialises in digital transformation, innovation design, and digital skills development, with activities spanning educational technology, educational research, and the design of digital skilling actions for diverse target groups — including public authorities, education providers, large enterprises, and SMEs at national and international level.

Why Our Consortium Works

Diverse Expertise, Shared Goals

Our consortium spans educational research, AI engineering, policy consulting, ethics, VET training, creative communication, and stakeholder relations. This intentional diversity ensures DIGI-METER is technically rigorous, ethically sound, pedagogically grounded, and practically applicable across different national education systems.

The geographic spread — from Cyprus to the Netherlands, from Portugal to Belgium — also means our tools are tested and validated in genuinely different cultural and institutional contexts, making them transferable across the EU and beyond.
