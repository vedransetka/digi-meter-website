About
DIGI-METER

An EU-funded research and development project translating the DigComp 2.2 framework into practical, evidence-based digital competence assessment tools for European schools and VET providers.

The Challenge

Why DIGI-METER Exists

While the DigComp 2.2 framework clearly defines the digital competences citizens need in today's world, its practical use in European schools and vocational education and training remains fragmented. Schools lack reliable tools to measure where learners actually stand. Teachers struggle to connect framework descriptors to everyday classroom realities. Policymakers cannot compare performance data across institutions and borders.

DIGI-METER bridges this gap. By translating DigComp 2.2 into measurable indicators, AI-supported assessment tools, and clear dashboards, the project equips every stakeholder — learner, educator, institution, and policymaker — with actionable insight into digital competence development.

Our Approach

Evidence-Based, Ethics-First

DIGI-METER develops AI-supported self-assessment tools, educator dashboards, and policy-ready indicator frameworks — all built on a foundation of rigorous research, inclusive design, and responsible AI principles. Our approach is grounded in real classrooms, validated across countries, and designed to grow alongside learners.

Ethics by Design

Every DIGI-METER tool is built with transparency, fairness, GDPR compliance, and explainability as core design requirements — not afterthoughts. Learners always retain control over their data.

Project Objectives

What We Set Out to Achieve

Five core objectives guide the DIGI-METER project. Final wording will be aligned with the approved project application.

01

Translate DigComp 2.2 into Measurable Indicators

Turn the DigComp 2.2 framework into measurable indicators and practical assessment tools for schools and vocational education and training.

02

Develop AI-Supported Self-Assessment Tools

Develop AI-supported self-assessment tools that give learners clear, personalised feedback on their digital competences.

03

Create a Teacher Dashboard & Open Resources

Provide a teacher dashboard and open resources that help schools, VET providers, and policymakers understand, monitor, and improve digital skills.

04

Embed Ethics, Inclusion & Data Protection by Design

Follow an ethics-by-design approach with attention to transparency, GDPR compliance, fairness, explainability, and user control, so that digital assessment is trustworthy, human-centred, and accessible to diverse learners.

05

Validate Across European Contexts & Inform Policy

Test and validate the tools across European contexts, supporting learners, teachers, education providers, and policy stakeholders in strengthening digital competence across Europe.

The EU Framework

Understanding DigComp 2.2

DigComp 2.2 is the European Digital Competence Framework for Citizens, developed by the Joint Research Centre of the European Commission. It describes 21 digital competences across 5 areas and 8 proficiency levels — from Foundation (Level 1) to Highly Specialised (Level 8). DIGI-METER operationalises this framework into measurable, classroom-ready indicators.

Area Focus Key Competences
Area 1 Information & Data Literacy Browsing, searching, filtering; Evaluating data; Managing data
Area 2 Communication & Collaboration Interacting; Sharing; Engaging citizenship; Collaborating; Netiquette; Managing digital identity
Area 3 Digital Content Creation Developing content; Integrating and re-elaborating; Copyright and licences; Programming
Area 4 Safety Protecting devices; Protecting personal data; Protecting health and well-being; Protecting the environment
Area 5 Problem Solving Solving technical problems; Identifying needs; Creatively using digital tools; Identifying digital competence gaps

Who Benefits

DIGI-METER Serves Four Key Groups

Learners

Personalised self-assessment results and feedback helping them understand where they are and what to focus on next in their digital competence journey.

Educators

Class-level dashboards showing aggregate competence data, supporting lesson planning, CPD decisions, and early identification of students needing targeted support.

Institutions

School and VET-centre level reporting enabling institutional leaders to benchmark digital competence levels, demonstrate progress, and plan strategic interventions.

Policymakers

System-level, cross-country aggregated data and evidence-based policy briefs to inform national digital education strategies and EU-level digital competence policy.
